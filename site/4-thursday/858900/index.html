    Sovereignty-How Does It Affect You?¶

    References¶

    • it-2 1008-1012

    Questions¶

    1. Define sovereignty. What other word conveys the underlying idea of sovereignty? (See 2 Chronicles 20:6 and references under Sovereignty in the Reference Bible Footnote Words Indexed)
    2. Give reasons why Jehovah is the rightful Universal Sovereign. (Job 41:11; Ps 24:1; Re 4:11)
    3. How do God’s qualities inspire confidence in his sovereignty? (De 32:4; 1Jo 4:8)
    4. How was Jehovah's sovereignty challenged? What was challenged and what was not challenged?
    5. What questions would have been unanswered if God had chosen to settle in Eden?
    6. What further challenge does the book of Job reveal?
    7. Highlight key points from Insight on the Scriptures, Volume 2, “Sovereignty.”
      a) “Earthly Rulers” and “God's Son as King”
      b) “Jehovah's Sovereignty Challenged” and “What God wants in those who serve him”
      c) “A failure to develop love and appreciation”
      d) “The point at issue” and “What was ‘the tree of the knowledge of good and bad’?”
      e) “God's servants charged with selfishness” and “Settlement of the issue, a vital need”
      f) “A moral issue” and “How far the issue reached”
      g) “Jesus Christ, Vindicator of the righteousness of Jehovah’s sovereignty” and “Others who uphold Jehovah’s side”
    8. Explain opportunities you have to demonstrate your attitude toward Jehovah's sovereignty, as indicated in the following scriptures. (Mt 24:45-47; 1Co 5:9-13; 7:39; Heb 13:17)
    9. Describe “loyalty” as it is used in the Bible.
    10. How is it possible for imperfect humans to prove that they are truly loyal to Jehovah? (Ge 6:22; 12:4; Ex 7:6; Da 3:14-18)
    11. Why must we constantly be on the alert as to our attitude toward Jehovah’s sovereignty?
    12. At the end of Christ’s Thousand Year Reign, what test will come on all humankind? (Re 20:7-9)
    13. Why will the issue of universal sovereignty never need to be settled again? (1Co 15:24-28)
