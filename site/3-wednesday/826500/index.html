    Sermon on the Mount-Part 1¶

    References¶

    • Mt 5:1-48
    • Lu 6:20-36

    Questions¶

    1. What did Jesus imply when he said his disciples were to be the "salt of the earth"? (Mt 5:13)
    2. In what two ways are Christians a saltlike influence?
    3. How could a Christian lose his strength as symbolic salt?
    4. In what two ways are Jesus’ disciples the “light of the world”? (Mt 5:14)
    5. In what sense will a person who breaks God’s commandments “be called least in relation to the Kingdom of the heavens”? (Mt 5:19)
    6. Why would persons who kept God's commandments “be called great in relation to the Kingdom of the heavens"? (Mt 5:19)
    7. What did Jesus mean when he said, “You must accordingly be perfect, as your heavenly Father is perfect’’? (Mt 5:48 ftn.)
